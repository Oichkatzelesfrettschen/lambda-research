# Comprehensive Academic Bibliography: Linear Lambda Calculus and Substructural Type Systems

### Girard's Seminal Work
1. **Girard, Jean-Yves** (1987). "Linear logic." *Theoretical Computer Science*, 50(1):1-101. DOI: 10.1016/0304-3975(87)90045-4
   - **Key Contribution**: Introduces linear logic as a refinement of classical and intuitionistic logic, establishing the theoretical foundation for resource-conscious computation
   - **Venue**: Theoretical Computer Science, Elsevier

2. **Girard, Jean-Yves** (1995). "Linear logic: its syntax and semantics." In *Advances in Linear Logic*, London Mathematical Society Lecture Note Series 222, pp. 1-42. Cambridge University Press.
   - **Key Contribution**: Comprehensive treatment of linear logic syntax and semantics
   - **Venue**: Cambridge University Press


2. Linear Lambda Calculus Foundational Papers



### Computational Interpretations
3. **Abramsky, Samson** (1993). "Computational interpretations of linear logic." *Theoretical Computer Science*, 111(1-2):3-57.
   - **Key Contribution**: Provides computational interpretation of linear logic based on Curry-Howard correspondence, leading to concurrent process paradigm
   - **Venue**: Theoretical Computer Science

4. **Benton, Nick, Gavin Bierman, Valeria de Paiva, and Martin Hyland** (1992). "Term assignment for intuitionistic linear logic." Technical Report 262, University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory.
   - **Key Contribution**: First systematic term assignment for intuitionistic linear logic
   - **Venue**: Cambridge Computer Laboratory Technical Report

5. **Benton, Nick, Gavin Bierman, Valeria de Paiva, and Martin Hyland** (1993). "A term calculus for intuitionistic linear logic." In *Proceedings of TLCA '93*, LNCS 664, pp. 75-90. Springer.
   - **Key Contribution**: Develops term calculus for intuitionistic linear logic with proof normalization
   - **Venue**: TLCA 1993



### Resource-Based Calculi
6. **Abramsky, Samson and Radha Jagadeesan** (1994). "New foundations for the geometry of interaction." *Information and Computation*, 111(1):53-119.
   - **Key Contribution**: Categorical foundations for geometry of interaction
   - **Venue**: Information and Computation


3. Affine Lambda Calculus and Resource-Aware Computation



### Substructural Lambda Calculi
7. **Ghilezan, Silvia, Jelena Ivetić, Pierre Lescanne, and Silvia Likavec** (2020). "Computational adequacy for substructural lambda calculi." *arXiv:2005.05433v2*.
   - **Key Contribution**: Formulates abstract categorical models for linear and affine type systems that are sound and computationally adequate
   - **Venue**: arXiv preprint

8. **Gundry, Adam Michael** (2021). "L-types for resource awareness: an implicit name approach." *arXiv:2112.11062*.
   - **Key Contribution**: First formalization of variable control in calculi with implicit names based on de Bruijn indices, introducing L-types for resource-aware calculi
   - **Venue**: arXiv preprint

9. **Ahmed, Amal, Matthew Fluet, and Greg Morrisett** (2005). "A step-indexed model of substructural state." *ACM SIGPLAN Notices*, 40(9):78-91.
   - **Key Contribution**: Semantic model for substructural state using step-indexed logical relations
   - **Venue**: ICFP 2005



### Practical Affine Types
10. **Tov, Jesse A. and Riccardo Pucella** (2011). "Practical affine types." *ACM SIGPLAN Notices*, 46(1):447-458.
    - **Key Contribution**: Model based on substructural variant of polymorphic λ-calculus with four kinds of mutable references: unrestricted, relevant, affine, and linear
    - **Venue**: POPL 2011


4. Relevant Lambda Calculus and Relevance Logic



### Dunn's Information Semantics
11. **Dunn, J. Michael** (1986). "Relevance logic and entailment." In *Handbook of Philosophical Logic*, Volume III, pp. 117-224. D. Reidel.
    - **Key Contribution**: Information-based interpretation of relevance logic with ternary relation semantics
    - **Venue**: Handbook of Philosophical Logic

12. **Anderson, Alan Ross, Nuel D. Belnap, and J. Michael Dunn** (1992). *Entailment: The Logic of Relevance and Necessity*, Volume II. Princeton University Press.
    - **Key Contribution**: Comprehensive treatment of relevance logic and its applications
    - **Venue**: Princeton University Press



### Resource Lambda Calculus
13. **Ehrhard, Thomas and Laurent Regnier** (2003). "The differential lambda-calculus." *Theoretical Computer Science*, 309(1-3):1-41.
    - **Key Contribution**: Extension of λ-calculus allowing to model resource consumption with differential operators
    - **Venue**: Theoretical Computer Science
