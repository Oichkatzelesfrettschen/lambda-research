# Untyped Lambda Calculus - Academic Bibliography


Foundational Works

**Church, A.** (1936). *An Unsolvable Problem of Elementary Number Theory*. American Journal of Mathematics, 58(2), 345-363.
- **Key Contribution**: First introduction of lambda calculus concepts and establishes undecidability results
- **DOI**: 10.2307/2371045

**Church, A.** (1941). *The Calculi of Lambda-Conversion*. Annals of Mathematics Studies, vol. 6. Princeton University Press.
- **Key Contribution**: The definitive foundational text on lambda calculus

**Kleene, S.C. & Rosser, J.B.** (1935). *The Inconsistency of Certain Formal Logics*. Annals of Mathematics, 36(3), 630-636.
- **Key Contribution**: Kleene-Rosser paradox showing inconsistency of original system
- **DOI**: 10.2307/1968646

**Curry, H.B. & Feys, R.** (1958). *Combinatory Logic, Volume I*. North-Holland Publishing Company.
- **Key Contribution**: Fundamental work connecting combinatory logic and lambda calculus

**Kleene, S.C.** (1936). *λ-definability and recursiveness*. Duke Mathematical Journal, 2(2), 340-353.
- **Key Contribution**: Establishes connection between lambda-definable and recursive functions
- **DOI**: 10.1215/S0012-7094-36-00227-2


Semantic Foundations

**Scott, D.S.** (1970). *Outline of a Mathematical Theory of Computation*. Oxford University Computing Laboratory, Technical Monograph PRG-2.
- **Key Contribution**: First successful model of untyped lambda calculus using domain theory

**Scott, D.S.** (1972). *Continuous Lattices*. Lecture Notes in Mathematics, 274, 97-136. Springer-Verlag.
- **Key Contribution**: Mathematical foundations for lambda calculus semantics

**Scott, D.S. & Strachey, C.** (1971). *Toward a Mathematical Semantics for Computer Languages*. Oxford University Computing Laboratory, Technical Monograph PRG-6.
- **Key Contribution**: Foundational work on denotational semantics

**Plotkin, G.D.** (1975). *Call-by-name, call-by-value and the lambda-calculus*. Theoretical Computer Science, 1(2), 125-159.
- **Key Contribution**: Fundamental analysis of evaluation strategies
- **DOI**: 10.1016/0304-3975(75)90017-1

**Wadsworth, C.P.** (1976). *The relation between computational and denotational properties for Scott's D∞-models of the lambda-calculus*. SIAM Journal on Computing, 5(3), 488-521.
- **Key Contribution**: Key work connecting operational and denotational semantics
- **DOI**: 10.1137/0205036


Comprehensive Textbooks

**Barendregt, H.P.** (1984). *The Lambda Calculus: Its Syntax and Semantics*. Studies in Logic and Foundations of Mathematics, vol. 103. North-Holland.
- **Key Contribution**: The definitive modern reference on lambda calculus (615 pp., revised edition)

**Hindley, J.R. & Seldin, J.P.** (2008). *Lambda-Calculus and Combinators: An Introduction*. Cambridge University Press, 2nd edition.
- **Key Contribution**: Comprehensive introduction with historical perspective

**Hankin, C.** (1994). *Lambda Calculi: A Guide for Computer Scientists*. Oxford University Press.
- **Key Contribution**: Practical introduction for computer science applications


Reduction Theory and Confluence

**Church, A. & Rosser, J.B.** (1936). *Some Properties of Conversion*. Transactions of the American Mathematical Society, 39(3), 472-482.
- **Key Contribution**: Church-Rosser theorem establishing confluence
- **DOI**: 10.2307/1989762

**Rosen, B.K.** (1973). *Tree-manipulating systems and Church-Rosser theorems*. Journal of the ACM, 20(1), 160-187.
- **Key Contribution**: General techniques for proving confluence
- **DOI**: 10.1145/321738.321750

**Klop, J.W.** (1980). *Combinatory Reduction Systems*. Mathematical Centre Tracts 127, CWI, Amsterdam.
- **Key Contribution**: PhD thesis - comprehensive treatment of reduction systems


Computational Complexity

**Statman, R.** (1979). *The typed λ-calculus is not elementary recursive*. Theoretical Computer Science, 9(1), 73-81.
- **Key Contribution**: Important complexity results for lambda calculus
- **DOI**: 10.1016/0304-3975(79)90007-0

**Schwichtenberg, H.** (1976). *Complexity of normalization in the pure typed lambda calculus*. In: The L.E.J. Brouwer Centenary Symposium, pp. 453-457.
- **Key Contribution**: Early complexity analysis of normalization


Historical and Philosophical

**Cardone, F. & Hindley, J.R.** (2006). *History of Lambda-calculus and Combinatory Logic*. In: Handbook of the History of Logic, Vol. 5, pp. 723-817. Elsevier.
- **Key Contribution**: Comprehensive historical survey

**Davis, M.** (1982). *Computability and Unsolvability*. Dover Publications (reprint of 1958 McGraw-Hill edition).
- **Key Contribution**: Historical context and foundational importance


Modern Developments

**Asperti, A. & Laneve, C.** (1993). *Interaction systems I: The theory of optimal reductions*. Mathematical Structures in Computer Science, 4(4), 457-504.
- **Key Contribution**: Modern approach to optimal reduction strategies
- **DOI**: 10.1017/S0960129500000547

**Lévy, J.-J.** (1978). *Réductions correctes et optimales dans le lambda-calcul*. Thèse de Doctorat d'État, Université Paris VII.
- **Key Contribution**: Foundational work on optimal reduction

**Lamping, J.** (1990). *An algorithm for optimal lambda calculus reduction*. Proceedings of the 17th ACM SIGPLAN-SIGACT Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages.
- **Key Contribution**: Practical algorithms for optimal reduction
- **DOI**: 10.1145/96709.96711

**Danos, V. & Regnier, L.** (1989). *The structure of multiplicatives*. Archive for Mathematical Logic, 28(3), 181-203.
- **Key Contribution**: Connection to linear logic and geometry of interaction
- **DOI**: 10.1007/BF01622878

**Abramsky, S. & Ong, C.-H.L.** (1993). *Full abstraction in the lazy lambda calculus*. Information and Computation, 105(2), 159-267.
- **Key Contribution**: Game semantics approach to lambda calculus
- **DOI**: 10.1006/inco.1993.1044
