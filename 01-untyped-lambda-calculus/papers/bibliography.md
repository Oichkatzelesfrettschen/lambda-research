# Untyped Lambda Calculus - Academic Bibliography

## Foundational Works

### 1. Church, Alonzo (1936)
**"An Unsolvable Problem of Elementary Number Theory"**
- American Journal of Mathematics 58(2): 345-363
- First introduction of lambda calculus concepts
- Establishes undecidability results

### 2. Church, Alonzo (1941)
**"The Calculi of Lambda-Conversion"**
- Annals of Mathematics Studies, no. 6
- Princeton University Press, Princeton
- 77 pp. (second printing 1951, 82 pp.)
- The definitive foundational text on lambda calculus

### 3. Kleene, Stephen Cole & Rosser, J. Barkley (1935)
**"The Inconsistency of Certain Formal Logics"**
- Annals of Mathematics 36(3): 630-636
- Kleene-Rosser paradox showing inconsistency of original system

### 4. Curry, Haskell B. & Feys, Robert (1958)
**"Combinatory Logic, Volume I"**
- North-Holland Publishing Company, Amsterdam
- Fundamental work connecting combinatory logic and lambda calculus

### 5. Kleene, Stephen Cole (1936)
**"λ-definability and recursiveness"**
- Duke Mathematical Journal 2(2): 340-353
- Establishes connection between lambda-definable and recursive functions

## Semantic Foundations

### 6. Scott, Dana S. (1970)
**"Outline of a Mathematical Theory of Computation"**
- Oxford University Computing Laboratory, Technical Monograph PRG-2
- First successful model of untyped lambda calculus using domain theory

### 7. Scott, Dana S. (1972)
**"Continuous Lattices"**
- Lecture Notes in Mathematics 274: 97-136, Springer-Verlag
- Mathematical foundations for lambda calculus semantics

### 8. Scott, Dana S. & Strachey, Christopher (1971)
**"Toward a Mathematical Semantics for Computer Languages"**
- Oxford University Computing Laboratory, Technical Monograph PRG-6
- Foundational work on denotational semantics

### 9. Plotkin, Gordon D. (1975)
**"Call-by-name, call-by-value and the lambda-calculus"**
- Theoretical Computer Science 1(2): 125-159
- Fundamental analysis of evaluation strategies

### 10. Wadsworth, Christopher P. (1976)
**"The relation between computational and denotational properties for Scott's D∞-models of the lambda-calculus"**
- SIAM Journal on Computing 5(3): 488-521
- Key work connecting operational and denotational semantics

## Comprehensive Textbooks

### 11. Barendregt, Henk P. (1984)
**"The Lambda Calculus: Its Syntax and Semantics"**
- Studies in Logic and Foundations of Mathematics, vol. 103
- North-Holland, Amsterdam (revised edition)
- 615 pp. - The definitive modern reference

### 12. Hindley, J. Roger & Seldin, Jonathan P. (2008)
**"Lambda-Calculus and Combinators: An Introduction"**
- Cambridge University Press, 2nd edition
- Comprehensive introduction with historical perspective

### 13. Hankin, Chris (1994)
**"Lambda Calculi: A Guide for Computer Scientists"**
- Oxford University Press
- Practical introduction for computer science applications

## Reduction Theory and Confluence

### 14. Church, Alonzo & Rosser, J. Barkley (1936)
**"Some Properties of Conversion"**
- Transactions of the American Mathematical Society 39(3): 472-482
- Church-Rosser theorem establishing confluence

### 15. Rosen, Barry K. (1973)
**"Tree-manipulating systems and Church-Rosser theorems"**
- Journal of the ACM 20(1): 160-187
- General techniques for proving confluence

### 16. Klop, Jan Willem (1980)
**"Combinatory Reduction Systems"**
- Mathematical Centre Tracts 127, CWI, Amsterdam
- PhD thesis - comprehensive treatment of reduction systems

## Computational Complexity

### 17. Statman, Richard (1979)
**"The typed λ-calculus is not elementary recursive"**
- Theoretical Computer Science 9(1): 73-81
- Important complexity results for lambda calculus

### 18. Schwichtenberg, Helmut (1976)
**"Complexity of normalization in the pure typed lambda calculus"**
- In: The L.E.J. Brouwer Centenary Symposium, pp. 453-457
- Early complexity analysis of normalization

## Historical and Philosophical

### 19. Cardone, Felice & Hindley, J. Roger (2006)
**"History of Lambda-calculus and Combinatory Logic"**
- In: Handbook of the History of Logic, Vol. 5, pp. 723-817
- Elsevier - Comprehensive historical survey

### 20. Davis, Martin (1982)
**"Computability and Unsolvability"**
- Dover Publications (reprint of 1958 McGraw-Hill edition)
- Historical context and foundational importance

## Modern Developments

### 21. Asperti, Andrea & Laneve, Cosimo (1993)
**"Interaction systems I: The theory of optimal reductions"**
- Mathematical Structures in Computer Science 4(4): 457-504
- Modern approach to optimal reduction strategies

### 22. Lévy, Jean-Jacques (1978)
**"Réductions correctes et optimales dans le lambda-calcul"**
- Thèse de Doctorat d'État, Université Paris VII
- Foundational work on optimal reduction

### 23. Lamping, John (1990)
**"An algorithm for optimal lambda calculus reduction"**
- Proceedings of the 17th ACM SIGPLAN-SIGACT Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages
- Practical algorithms for optimal reduction

### 24. Danos, Vincent & Regnier, Laurent (2004)
**"The structure of multiplicatives"**
- Archive for Mathematical Logic 28(3): 181-203
- Connection to linear logic and geometry of interaction

### 25. Abramsky, Samson & Ong, C.-H. Luke (1993)
**"Full abstraction in the lazy lambda calculus"**
- Information and Computation 105(2): 159-267
- Game semantics approach to lambda calculus
