# Comprehensive Academic Bibliography: Martin-Löf Type Theory (MLTT)

A comprehensive collection of foundational and contemporary papers on Martin-Löf Type Theory, organized by topic and spanning from the original works of the 1970s to modern developments through 2024-2025.

## 1. Per Martin-Löf's Foundational Works (1970s-1980s)

**Martin-Löf, Per** (1975). "An intuitionistic theory of types: predicative part." In *Logic Colloquium '73*, edited by H.E. Rose and J.C. Shepherdson, 73-118. Amsterdam: North-Holland.
- **Key contribution**: First systematic presentation of intuitionistic type theory with dependent types
- **Venue**: Logic Colloquium proceedings

**Martin-Löf, Per** (1982). "Constructive Mathematics and Computer Programming." In *Logic, methodology, and philosophy of science VI: proceedings of the Sixth International Congress of Logic, Methodology, and Philosophy of Science*, edited by Laurence Jonathan Cohen, 153-175. New York: Elsevier North-Holland.
- **Key contribution**: Established the foundational relationship between constructive mathematics and programming through type theory
- **Venue**: International Congress proceedings (originally presented 1979)

**Martin-Löf, Per** (1984). *Intuitionistic type theory: Notes by Giovanni Sambin of a series of lectures given in Padua, June 1980*. Studies in proof theory. Napoli: Bibliopolis. ix + 91 pp.
- **Key contribution**: Definitive presentation of Martin-Löf's intuitionistic type theory including dependent types, identity types, and universes
- **Venue**: Monograph (Bibliopolis)

## 2. Major Contributions to Constructive Type Theory

**Nordström, Bengt, Kent Petersson, and Jan M. Smith** (1990). *Programming in Martin-Löf's Type Theory: An Introduction*. Oxford: Clarendon Press.
- **Key contribution**: First comprehensive practical guide to programming and proving in Martin-Löf's type theory
- **Venue**: Monograph (Oxford University Press)

**Coquand, Thierry and Gérard Huet** (1988). "The calculus of constructions." *Information and Computation* 76(2-3): 95-120.
- **Key contribution**: Introduced the Calculus of Constructions, combining higher-order polymorphism with dependent types
- **Venue**: Information and Computation

**Dybjer, Peter** (1994). "Inductive families." *Formal Aspects of Computing* 6(4): 440-465.
- **Key contribution**: Systematic treatment of inductive families and their role in dependent type theory
- **Venue**: Formal Aspects of Computing

**Dybjer, Peter** (2000). "A general formulation of simultaneous inductive-recursive definitions in type theory." *Journal of Symbolic Logic* 65(2): 525-549.
- **Key contribution**: Formalized inductive-recursive definitions, extending the expressive power of type theory
- **Venue**: Journal of Symbolic Logic

## 3. Intuitionistic Type Theory Papers

**Beeson, Michael J.** (1985). *Foundations of Constructive Mathematics: Metamathematical Studies*. Berlin: Springer-Verlag.
- **Key contribution**: Comprehensive treatment of constructive mathematics foundations including type-theoretic approaches
- **Venue**: Monograph (Springer-Verlag)

**Aczel, Peter** (1978). "The type theoretic interpretation of constructive set theory." In *Logic Colloquium '77*, edited by A. MacIntyre, L. Pacholski, and J. Paris, 55-66. Amsterdam: North-Holland.
- **Key contribution**: Bridge between constructive set theory and type theory
- **Venue**: Logic Colloquium proceedings

**Troelstra, Anne S. and Dirk van Dalen** (1988). *Constructivism in Mathematics: An Introduction*. Amsterdam: North-Holland.
- **Key contribution**: Comprehensive introduction to constructive mathematics including type-theoretic foundations
- **Venue**: Monograph (North-Holland)

## 4. Identity Types and Equality

**Hofmann, Martin and Thomas Streicher** (1998). "The groupoid interpretation of type theory." In *Twenty-five years of constructive type theory (Venice, 1995)*, Oxford Logic Guides, vol. 36, 83-111. New York: Oxford University Press.
- **Key contribution**: First non-trivial model of identity types, showing that uniqueness of identity proofs is not derivable
- **Venue**: Oxford Logic Guides

**Awodey, Steve and Michael A. Warren** (2009). "Homotopy theoretic models of identity types." *Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society* 146(1): 45-55.
- **Key contribution**: Established the connection between identity types and homotopy theory
- **Venue**: Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society

**Voevodsky, Vladimir** (2006). "A very short note on homotopy λ-calculus." Unpublished note.
- **Key contribution**: Early insights connecting homotopy theory and type theory, leading to univalent foundations
- **Venue**: Unpublished note

## 5. Universe Hierarchies

**Martin-Löf, Per** (1975). "An intuitionistic theory of types: predicative part." (See above)
- **Key contribution**: Introduction of universes à la Tarski in type theory
- **Venue**: Logic Colloquium proceedings

**Sterling, Jonathan** (2019). "Algebraic Type Theory and Universe Hierarchies." arXiv:1902.08848.
- **Key contribution**: Clarified the relationship between Russell and Tarski style universes in algebraic type theory
- **Venue**: arXiv preprint

**Kovács, András** (2022). "Generalized Universe Hierarchies and First-Class Universe Levels." In *Computer Science Logic (CSL 2022)*, edited by F. Manea and A. Simpson. LIPIcs 216: 28:1-28:17.
- **Key contribution**: Advanced treatment of universe hierarchies with first-class universe levels
- **Venue**: Computer Science Logic

**Palmgren, Erik** (1998). "On universes in type theory." In *Twenty-five years of constructive type theory*, Oxford Logic Guides, vol. 36, 191-204. Oxford University Press.
- **Key contribution**: Systematic study of various universe constructions and their proof-theoretic strength
- **Venue**: Oxford Logic Guides

## 6. Dependent Types Foundations

**de Bruijn, Nicolaas G.** (1970). "The mathematical language AUTOMATH, its usage, and some of its extensions." In *Symposium on Automatic Demonstration*, Lecture Notes in Mathematics 125, 29-61. Springer-Verlag.
- **Key contribution**: First practical implementation of dependent types in the AUTOMATH system
- **Venue**: Symposium proceedings (Springer)

**Barendregt, Henk** (1991). "Introduction to generalized type systems." *Journal of Functional Programming* 1(2): 125-154.
- **Key contribution**: Systematic classification of type systems including the lambda cube framework
- **Venue**: Journal of Functional Programming

**Church, Alonzo** (1932-1933). "A set of postulates for the foundation of logic." *Annals of Mathematics* 33: 346-366 (Part 1), 34: 839-864 (Part 2).
- **Key contribution**: Original lambda calculus foundations underlying modern type theory
- **Venue**: Annals of Mathematics

## 7. Categorical Semantics

**Seely, R.A.G.** (1984). "Locally cartesian closed categories and type theory." *Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society* 95(1): 33-48.
- **Key contribution**: Established the connection between locally cartesian closed categories and dependent type theory
- **Venue**: Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society

**Clairambault, Pierre and Peter Dybjer** (2011). "The Biequivalence of Locally Cartesian Closed Categories and Martin-Löf Type Theories." In *Typed Lambda Calculi and Applications (TLCA 2011)*, Lecture Notes in Computer Science 6690, 91-106. Springer.
- **Key contribution**: Corrected Seely's theorem with a proper biequivalence between LCCCs and type theories
- **Venue**: Typed Lambda Calculi and Applications

**Jacobs, Bart** (1993). "Comprehension categories and the semantics of type dependency." *Journal of Symbolic Logic* 58(2): 493-514.
- **Key contribution**: Introduced comprehension categories as a framework for dependent type theory semantics
- **Venue**: Journal of Symbolic Logic

**Curien, Pierre-Louis, Richard Garner, and Martin Hofmann** (2014). "Revisiting the categorical interpretation of dependent type theory." *Theoretical Computer Science* 546: 99-119.
- **Key contribution**: Updated and refined the categorical semantics of dependent type theory
- **Venue**: Theoretical Computer Science

## 8. Modern Developments and Extensions

**Univalent Foundations Program** (2013). *Homotopy Type Theory: Univalent Foundations of Mathematics*. Princeton: Institute for Advanced Study.
- **Key contribution**: Comprehensive introduction to homotopy type theory and univalent foundations
- **Venue**: Monograph (IAS)

**Cohen, Cyril, Thierry Coquand, Simon Huber, and Anders Mörtberg** (2018). "Cubical Type Theory: A constructive interpretation of the univalence axiom." *IfCoLog Journal of Logics and their Applications* 4(10): 3127-3169.
- **Key contribution**: Made univalence computational through cubical type theory
- **Venue**: IfCoLog Journal

**Cavallo, Evan and Robert Harper** (2019). "Higher inductive types in cubical computational type theory." *Proceedings of the ACM on Programming Languages* 3(POPL): 1-27.
- **Key contribution**: Computational interpretation of higher inductive types in cubical type theory
- **Venue**: POPL

**Voevodsky, Vladimir** (2013). "A simple type system with two identity types." Unpublished note.
- **Key contribution**: Exploration of type systems with multiple notions of equality
- **Venue**: Unpublished note

## 9. Implementation Papers (Agda, Lean, etc.)

**Norell, Ulf** (2007). "Towards a practical programming language based on dependent type theory." PhD thesis, Chalmers University of Technology.
- **Key contribution**: Design and implementation of the Agda programming language and proof assistant
- **Venue**: PhD thesis (Chalmers)

**Bove, Ana, Peter Dybjer, and Ulf Norell** (2009). "A Brief Overview of Agda – A Functional Language with Dependent Types." In *Theorem Proving in Higher Order Logics (TPHOLs 2009)*, Lecture Notes in Computer Science 5674, 73-78. Springer.
- **Key contribution**: Introduction to Agda's dependent type system and its applications
- **Venue**: Theorem Proving in Higher Order Logics

**de Moura, Leonardo, Soonho Kong, Jeremy Avigad, Floris van Doorn, and Jakob von Raumer** (2015). "The Lean Theorem Prover (System Description)." In *Automated Deduction (CADE 2015)*, Lecture Notes in Computer Science 9195, 378-388. Springer.
- **Key contribution**: Introduction to the Lean theorem prover and its type theory foundations
- **Venue**: Conference on Automated Deduction

**de Moura, Leonardo and Sebastian Ullrich** (2021). "The Lean 4 Theorem Prover and Programming Language." In *Automated Deduction (CADE 2021)*, Lecture Notes in Computer Science 12699, 625-635. Springer.
- **Key contribution**: Major revision of Lean with improved foundations and performance
- **Venue**: Conference on Automated Deduction

**Vezzosi, Andrea, Anders Mörtberg, and Andreas Abel** (2019). "Cubical Agda: A dependently typed programming language with univalence and higher inductive types." *Journal of Functional Programming* 29: e8.
- **Key contribution**: Extension of Agda with cubical type theory primitives enabling computational univalence
- **Venue**: Journal of Functional Programming

## 10. Homotopy Type Theory Connections

**Awodey, Steve and Michael A. Warren** (2009). "Homotopy theoretic models of identity types." (See above)
- **Key contribution**: Foundational connection between homotopy theory and type theory
- **Venue**: Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society

**Lumsdaine, Peter LeFanu and Michael Shulman** (2017). "Semantics of higher inductive types." *Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society* 169(1): 159-208.
- **Key contribution**: Categorical semantics of higher inductive types using homotopy theory
- **Venue**: Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society

**Ljungström, Axel and Anders Mörtberg** (2023). "Formalizing π₄(S³) ≅ ℤ/2ℤ and Computing a Brunerie Number in Cubical Agda." In *Logic in Computer Science (LICS 2023)*, 1-13. IEEE.
- **Key contribution**: First complete formalization of a non-trivial homotopy group computation
- **Venue**: Logic in Computer Science (Distinguished Paper Award)

**Brunerie, Guillaume, Axel Ljungström, and Anders Mörtberg** (2022). "Synthetic Integral Cohomology in Cubical Agda." In *Computer Science Logic (CSL 2022)*, LIPIcs 216: 11:1-11:19.
- **Key contribution**: Computational approach to cohomology theory in homotopy type theory
- **Venue**: Computer Science Logic

**Licata, Daniel R. and Guillaume Brunerie** (2015). "A cubical approach to synthetic homotopy theory." In *Logic in Computer Science (LICS 2015)*, 92-103. IEEE.
- **Key contribution**: Systematic development of synthetic homotopy theory using cubical methods
- **Venue**: Logic in Computer Science

**Shulman, Michael** (2015). "Homotopy type theory: A synthetic approach to higher equalities." In *Categories for the Working Philosopher*, edited by Elaine Landry, 293-330. Oxford University Press.
- **Key contribution**: Philosophical and mathematical introduction to homotopy type theory
- **Venue**: Categories for the Working Philosopher
